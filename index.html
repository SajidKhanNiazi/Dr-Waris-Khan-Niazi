Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about psychiatric care and our practice.

What does a psychiatrist do?

A psychiatrist is a medical doctor who specializes in the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental health disorders. Unlike psychologists, psychiatrists can prescribe medications and offer a combination of therapy and medical treatment for conditions like depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, and bipolar disorder.

Is my information kept confidential?

Absolutely. Patient confidentiality is a cornerstone of our practice. All personal information, medical records, and details shared during sessions are kept strictly confidential in accordance with medical ethics and privacy regulations. Your trust and privacy are our top priority.

How do I book an appointment?

You can book an appointment by calling our clinic, reaching us via WhatsApp, or by filling out the appointment request form on this website. We will confirm your appointment and provide any additional instructions for your visit.

What should I bring to my first visit?

For your first visit, please bring any previous medical records, a list of current medications you are taking, your national ID card, and a brief written summary of your symptoms or concerns. This helps Dr. Niazi provide the most effective assessment and treatment plan.

Do you treat children and teenagers?

Yes. Dr. Niazi provides specialized psychiatric care for children and adolescents experiencing behavioral, emotional, or developmental challenges. Treatment approaches are adapted to be age-appropriate and are conducted with parental involvement for the best outcomes.

What is the difference between a psychiatrist and a psychologist?

A psychiatrist is a medical doctor (MBBS + specialization) who can diagnose mental illnesses, prescribe medications, and provide therapy. A psychologist holds a degree in psychology and primarily offers counseling and behavioral therapies but cannot prescribe medication. Both work together to provide comprehensive mental health care.
